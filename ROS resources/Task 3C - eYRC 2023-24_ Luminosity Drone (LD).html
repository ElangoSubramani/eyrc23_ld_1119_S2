Understanding LiPo Battery Safety: Charging and Testing

LiPo batteries are widely used in various electronic devices, including drones and RC vehicles, due to their high energy density. While these batteries offer excellent performance, it is crucial to handle them with care to ensure safety and longevity. Here, we'll cover essential aspects of LiPo battery charging and testing, focusing on the critical nature of LiPo battery safety.

Charging LiPo Batteries

  • Charger Specifications : When charging LiPo batteries, always use a charger specifically designed for LiPo cells. The Tattu 850mAh 3S 75C LiPo battery, for instance, is compatible with chargers capable of handling 3 cells (3S) and a maximum charge current of 1.2A.

  • Charger Operation :

    • Ensure the charger is in good condition.
    • Connect the battery correctly to avoid errors (indicated by flashing red LEDs).
    • Monitor the LED indicators throughout the charging process
Charger StatusLED Indicator
Ready to ChargeGreen LED flashes red
ChargingConstant red glow
Charging CompleteConstant green glow
  • Error Messages :
Charger StatusLED Indicator
Incorrect ConnectionAll LEDs flash red once
Overload or Short CircuitAll LEDs flash red twice
Voltage DiscrepancyAll LEDs flash red four times
Cell Voltage ImbalanceAll LEDs flash red six times

WARNING ⚠️: DO NOT leave the battery on charge in unattended. Although the charger is a balance charger and will stop charging the battery once fully charged, but the charger should not be kept on even after the battery is fully charged. Remove the battery from the charger after turning the charger off, or it may lead to draining of the battery cells

LiPo Battery Testing

A 1-8S LiPo Battery Voltage Tester is a valuable tool for monitoring the voltage of each cell in your LiPo battery pack.

Proper usage ensures you can identify imbalances between cells and prevent over-discharging, contributing to the safety and longevity of your batteries. Regularly test LiPo battery voltages, especially before and after use, using a 1-8S LiPo Battery Voltage Tester. For the Tattu 850mAh 3S 75C LiPo battery, the minimum safe voltage of LiPo cells should not drop below 3.0 volts per cell.

Ensure the correct orientation of the connector to avoid damage. Once connected, the voltage tester should display the voltage of each cell in the LiPo battery pack as well as the volatge of combined cells.

Warning ⚠️: A LiPo cell should NEVER be discharged below 3.0V. Discharging a LiPo cell lower than 3.3V causes a usually permanent degradation of the cell’s ability to absorb and retain a charge. Do not use the battery if any cell volatge goes below 3.3V. Proper LiPo Storage Voltage = 3.8V per cell. If the LiPo battery is not going to be used for long time, it must stored keeping all individual cell at around 3.8V.

Remember, safety is paramount. Charge and use LiPo batteries responsibly to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

Preparing SD Card for Raspberry Pi Zero

  • Download the Operating System Image: Download the image for Raspberry Pi Zero from the following link.

  • Unzip the Image File : Once the download is complete, unzip the downloaded file. This will give you an .img file.

  • Insert the SD Card: Insert the microSD card into your laptop or PC using a card reader or adapter. Ensure the SD card is properly connected.

  • Identify the SD Card: Open the disk utility on your computer. For example, on Linux, you can use the "Disks" application. Identify the SD card in the list of available disks. Take note of the disk identifier (e.g., /dev/sdX).

  • Burn the Image to the SD Card:

Open a terminal on your computer and use the dd command to write the image to the SD card. Be extremely careful with this step, as selecting the wrong disk may lead to data loss.

sudo dd if=/path/to/downloaded/image.img of=/dev/sdX status=progress

From Fig 4 we can see that X=a, so command goes like

sudo dd if=/path/to/downloaded/image.img of=/dev/sda status=progress

⚠️ Caution is paramount when using the sudo dd if=/path/to/downloaded/image.img of=/dev/sdX status=progress command. This powerful tool can lead to irreversible data loss if misused. Ensure absolute precision in identifying the target device (/dev/sdX), back up critical data, and double-check the device path to prevent accidental overwrites. There's no confirmation prompt, so be certain of your parameters before execution. Verify the image file's integrity to avoid writing corrupted data, and understand that interrupting the process may result in incomplete writes. Patience is key during the operation, and constant vigilance over the progress status is advised.

Arming and Disarming the Swift

Note❗: Do not proceed without watching the video carefully.

In this section, we'll guide you through the process of arming and disarming the drone using ROS2. Before proceeding make sure you have ROS2 installed by following the instructions in Task 3B.

  • Create your workspace or if you have an existing workspace, navigate to the workspace

    Note❗: In the video, don't get confuse with workspace name.

    cd
mkdir -p colcon_ws/src

  • Clone the swift_msgs repo

    cd colcon_ws
git clone https://github.com/erts-RnD/swift_msgs.git src

  • Build your workspace

    colcon build

  • Source your workspace

    cd colcon_ws
source install/setup.bash
echo "source install/setup.bash" >> ~/.bashrc

  • After these steps, create a WiFi hotspot from your laptop/pc with the following settings:

    SSID : swift

    Password : eyantra@123

    Refer to the images below for a visual guide:

  • Plug in the drone battery. You'll hear a sound, and two LEDs on the LED-Buzzer module will light up with different colors. The right LED indicates the battery status, and the left one will be red for the first 45 seconds.

    Before moving forward, take a look at different LED indicators.

    LED Indicator (Left)Battery Status
    RPi still booting
    RPi booted and connected to FC
    Drone is armed (Props rotating)

  • Arm the drone

    Caution ⚠️: Prior to arming the drone, ensure that the battery wires are free from entanglement. Additionally, during the arming process, hold the drone securely in your hand, grasping it from the bottom to prevent any accidents or damage to the device. Pay close attention to the instructional video.

    You could only arm the drone once the FC(Flight Controller) is connected to RPi i.e blue light on the right LED of the LED Buzzer module.

    cd colcon_ws
source install/setup.bash
ros2 service call /swift/cmd/arming swift_msgs/srv/CommandBool value:\ true\

    When arming the drone, the right LED will change from blue to green.

    After arming the drone check the direction of motors from fig 8.

  • Disarm the drone

    ros2 service call /swift/cmd/arming swift_msgs/srv/CommandBool value:\ false\

    Disarming the drone will change the right LED back to blue.

  • After arming the drone, you can check the Pi camera by running the following commands:

    git clone https://github.com/erts-RnD/rpi_cam.git
cd rpi_cam/python
python3 server.py
